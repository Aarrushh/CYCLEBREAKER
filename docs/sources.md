# Curated Sources (Initial)

Principles
- Prefer official government portals and foundation pages.
- Track ToS, robots policies, and any available APIs.

United States
- Grants.gov — federal grants (API available)
- SAM.gov — contract/grant notices
- Challenge.gov — federal challenges/prizes
- SBA.gov — small business programs
- State portals — e.g., CA GO-Biz, NYS Grants Gateway, Texas eGrants
- City/County portals — economic dev, workforce, CDBG/HOME notices
- Apprenticeship.gov — training/apprenticeships
- Department of Labor/Workforce boards — WIOA programs
- Philanthropy — major foundations (Gates, Knight, community foundations)

United Kingdom
- GOV.UK — grants and funding finder
- Innovate UK — funding competitions
- UKRI — research councils
- NIHR — health research
- Local councils — small business grants, community funds

European Union
- Funding & Tenders Portal — EU programmes
- Erasmus+ — education/training
- EIC — innovation

Canada
- GC Grants & Contributions
- Provincial programs — Ontario, BC, Quebec, etc.

Australia
- business.gov.au — grants finder
- State government portals

India
- MyScheme.gov.in (aggregator)
- State-specific portals (MSME, skill development, eduction)

Global/Multilateral
- World Bank, UNDP, UNICEF, UNHCR, ADB, AfDB, IDB

Notes
- Record source domains, last-checked timestamps, and adapter health.
- Where APIs exist, use them first; otherwise, focus on high-signal HTML pages (newsrooms, funding pages, open calls).
